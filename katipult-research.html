6 Case Study Breakdown

Each case study focuses on a specific problem, specific users, and specific outcome. Not "here's everything I did in 17 years."

1. Investor Onboarding (KYC/AML)

Problem: 45-minute onboarding with 60% drop-off at document upload

Solution: Progressive disclosure, save-and-resume, jurisdiction-aware rules

Result: 12 minutes, 85% completion rate

Users: Investors, Compliance Officers, Fund Managers

2. Deal Management Platform

Problem: Deal lifecycle managed through spreadsheets and email chains

Solution: End-to-end platform: create → fund → distribute

Result: 150+ offerings, $647M facilitated in 2023 (71% YoY)

Users: Issuers, Fund Managers, Platform Admins

3. Donations & Rewards Platform

Problem: Arts organizations and nonprofits had no branded fundraising platform

Solution: White-label crowdfunding with dual funding (donations + rewards)

Result: InvestYYC: $650K raised, 2,000 donors, 45 arts projects

Users: Donors, Arts Organizations, Platform Administrators

Client: Calgary Arts Development + ATB Financial

4. Lending Platform

Problem: Debt/lending workflows have fundamentally different UX from equity investment

Solution: Purpose-built lending module with debt-specific flows

Result: Extended platform from equity-only to full debt + equity coverage

Users: Lenders, Borrowers, Loan Administrators

5. White-Label Design System

Problem: Each client wanted their own branded investor portal — couldn't maintain separate codebases

Solution: Token-based design system: colors, logos, typography adapt per client

Result: One codebase serving 50+ client brands

Users: Client brand managers, designers, developers

Framing Rules (From Vendy)

CRITICAL — COPY FRAMING
  • DO: "Our team designed...", "We shipped...", "Working with 3 engineers and compliance, we reduced..."
  • DO: Lead with outcomes: "Onboarding dropped from 45 to 12 minutes" — then add team context
  • DON'T: "I designed...", "I built...", "I led..." — excessive first-person sounds self-centered
  • DON'T: "Katipult offers...", "The platform provides...", "Katipult is a..."
  • Each case study is about design leadership and team outcomes, not a Katipult marketing page or a solo hero story
  • Reference 1-3 specific enterprise clients per case study when possible
  • Keep it focused — one problem, one approach, one result per case study
CRITICAL — COLLABORATIVE TONE (VENDY DIRECTIVE, APRIL 9 2026)

Default voice order: Outcome first → Team second → Personal role third

  • Best: "Onboarding completion rose from 40% to 85% after our team redesigned the KYC flow with progressive disclosure and save-and-resume."
  • Good: "Our team reduced NIGO documents by 98% through intelligent form validation and real-time compliance checks."
  • OK (sparingly): "As Head of Product Design, I led the migration to a token-based design system — working with 2 designers and 4 engineers to ship 154 client implementations."
  • Bad: "I redesigned the onboarding flow. I reduced drop-off. I built the design system. I shipped it."

"I" is acceptable only for clear leadership decisions, always paired with team context. Default to "we" or "our team" or lead with the outcome itself.

Why: Hiring managers see "I did everything" as a red flag. Collaborative framing shows leadership maturity — the ability to build WITH teams. Pheak co-founded and led design for 17 years; the story is about team outcomes, not solo heroics.

Title Framing Rules (From Pheak)

CRITICAL — NO "CPO" OR "CHIEF" TITLES

Startup title inflation hurts credibility for senior IC design roles. Hiring managers see "CPO" at a 3-person startup and think "inflated" or "manager who doesn't design." Always use function-based titles.

2014–2020 (10-30 people)Lead Product DesignerLead Product DesignerAccurate — led design AND designed

Rules
  • Never use "CPO" or "Chief" anywhere in portfolio or resume
  • Lead with the design role, add co-founder as context
  • Describe what the team DID: "Our team designed every investor-facing screen" — not "I oversaw the design department"
  • Show outcomes next to problems (Vendy's recommendation — confirmed correct pattern for senior portfolios)

Private Lending Platform — ATB Financial (Deep Spec, from OneDrive)

Client: ATB Financial (Alberta Treasury Branches) — Crown corporation bank

Scope: 28-page platform specification for small business lending + microfinance

Key design decisions our team made:

  • KYC Integration: Trulioo (global identity verification) + Onfido (document + selfie verification) — designed flows for both manual and automated pathways
  • Investment Vehicles: TFSA, RRSP, cash accounts — each with different regulatory UX requirements
  • Auto-Invest Algorithm: Rules engine where investors set criteria by risk profile, time horizon, loan size, country, and industry — then system automatically allocates
  • E-Wallet Payment Processing: Integrated wallet system for deposit, withdrawal, and transfer flows
  • Digital Signatures: Designed both manual signature upload AND electronic signature flows
  • Three User Journeys: Lender (browse → invest → manage), Borrower (apply → fund → repay), Administrator (approve → monitor → report)
  • Guest Registration: Social login + progressive profile completion to reduce onboarding friction

Portfolio use: This is the strongest material for the Lending case study. Names real vendors (Trulioo, Onfido) that fintech hiring managers recognize. Shows our team designed complex financial product UX including Canadian-specific investment vehicles (TFSA/RRSP).
