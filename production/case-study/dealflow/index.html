PLATFORM OVERVIEW

THE INFRASTRUCTURE BEHIND PRIVATE CAPITAL

DealFlow is the core deal management module of Katipult's white-label platform. It handles the full lifecycle of private securities, from creating offerings and setting terms to managing investor allocations, compliance checks, and document generation, all within a multi-tenant architecture that serves 150+ enterprise clients simultaneously.

HOW I FRAMED IT

RESEARCH-DRIVEN DESIGN DECISIONS

Before designing any screens, I mapped every metric to a business problem and then to a user need. This framework ensured every design decision was traceable back to real pain points, not assumptions.

RESEARCH TABLE
METRIC BUSINESS PROBLEM USER NEED
Deal completion rate Only 40% of started deals reached full funding. Investors dropped off mid-process due to friction in the allocation workflow. Investors need a clear, step-by-step process that shows progress and reduces cognitive load during investment.
Time to close Average deal close took 45+ days. Manual document handling and compliance reviews created bottlenecks. Dealers need automated document generation and parallel compliance workflows to accelerate closings.
Multi-role adoption Platform served 5 distinct roles but had a single UI. Power users were overwhelmed; casual users couldn't find basic features. Each role needs a tailored dashboard showing only relevant actions, data, and navigation for their workflow.
Portfolio visibility Investors had no consolidated view of their holdings. Checking status required contacting dealers directly. Investors need a real-time portfolio dashboard with deal status, distributions, and document access in one place.
White-label scalability Each new client required 2-3 weeks of custom configuration. The platform couldn't scale beyond 50 simultaneous tenants. Admins need a self-service configuration system that handles branding, permissions, and workflow customization.
THE QUESTION

HOW MIGHT WE DESIGN IT?

The research made one thing clear: the platform needed to feel like five different products while running on one codebase. Each user role had fundamentally different mental models, workflows, and success metrics.

HOW MIGHT WE

Design a single deal management platform that adapts its interface, navigation, and data density to serve five distinct user roles, from first-time investors to compliance officers managing 20+ jurisdictions, without requiring separate codebases or training programs?

WHAT WE LOOKED AT

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

We analyzed four competing platforms to identify gaps and opportunities in the private capital market space.

IMS

Legacy desktop application with deep functionality but outdated interface. Strong compliance tools but poor investor-facing experience. No mobile support.

NIAD

Modern investor portal focused on retail crowdfunding. Clean interface but limited to simple equity deals. No multi-role support or white-label capabilities.

DEALPOINT

Enterprise deal management with strong reporting. Good for administrators but overwhelming for investors. Steep learning curve with no progressive disclosure.

PARVIS

Canadian platform focused on real estate private equity. Polished investor experience but narrow asset class support. Single-jurisdiction compliance only.

INVESTOR DASHBOARD

PORTFOLIO AT A GLANCE

The investor dashboard consolidates holdings, active deals, and document access into a single view. Investors see their portfolio performance, pending allocations, and compliance status without navigating away from the main screen.

DESIGN DECISION

We prioritized a summary-first approach: key metrics and deal status visible immediately, with progressive disclosure for detailed allocation data and historical transactions.

EQUITY & DEBT

BROWSE, COMPARE, INVEST

The offerings browser handles both equity and debt instruments with unified navigation. Investors can filter by asset class, risk profile, and minimum investment, then drill into individual deal terms, documents, and allocation history.

DESIGN DECISION

Card-based offerings with key metrics visible at scan level. Investors compare deals without opening individual pages, reducing the time from discovery to investment commitment.
