THE CHALLENGE

RANDOM SELECTION IS BORING. WHAT IF IT WASN’T?

Every classroom, meeting, and game night uses the same tired tools: spinners, dice rolls, name pickers. They work, but they’re forgettable. There’s no anticipation, no shared experience, no moment worth watching.

I wanted to build something that makes random selection feel like an event. An elimination race where you watch your pick compete in real time, cheer as others get knocked out, and share the drama with everyone in the room.

The design challenge: make it instantly understandable, visually exciting, and playable by anyone from a 10-year-old to a CEO.

DESIGN APPROACH

GAME UX MEETS UTILITY

The core insight: a randomizer doesn’t need to feel like a tool. It needs to feel like a game. I studied elimination-style games (Mario Kart, Fall Guys, Survivor) to understand what creates tension.

The answer: progressive elimination plus a camera that follows the leader. When the field narrows and the camera tightens, even a random outcome feels earned.

MULTIPLAYER

REAL-TIME SYNC ACROSS EVERY DEVICE

The multiplayer system uses an authoritative host pattern over Supabase Realtime. One host runs the race; viewers receive state updates and render locally.
