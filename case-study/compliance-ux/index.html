Deep Problem Framing
The Goal

Our team redesigned the investor onboarding flow that was causing 60% drop-off at the document upload step. Each failed verification cost $200+ in manual compliance review. The challenge: reduce a 45-minute process to under 15 minutes while maintaining KYC/AML compliance across 20+ regulatory jurisdictions.

The Problem

Investors uploading sensitive ID documents abandoned at alarming rates. The flow asked for everything upfront — 6-8 documents in a single session with no save-and-resume. Compliance officers had zero visibility into who was stuck vs. who had abandoned. Different jurisdictions required different documents but the UI treated them identically. Identity verification integrations with providers like Trulioo and Onfido were fragmented and poorly surfaced in the flow.

How Might We

How might we reduce investor onboarding from 45 to under 15 minutes — cutting drop-off from 60% to 15% while maintaining compliance across 20+ jurisdictions?

Before 45 min · 60% drop-off $200+ per failed verification. No save-and-resume. One-size-fits-all across jurisdictions.
After 12 min · 85% completion Progressive steps with save-and-resume. Jurisdiction-aware rules engine.
Scale 20+ jurisdictions 150+ completed offerings across IIROC, Reg CF, MiFID, and FINTRAC jurisdictions.
User Research
Methodology

Stakeholder interviews with compliance officers, fund managers, and investors across 5 jurisdictions. Observation sessions during live onboarding. Analytics review of drop-off points.

Sample

12 compliance officers, 8 fund managers, 20+ investors. Multiple rounds across platform iterations over 6+ years of continuous research.

Key Insights
Document fatigue kills completion

Investors uploading 6-8 documents in one session abandoned at 60% rate. Breaking into progressive steps with save-and-resume changed everything.

Jurisdiction complexity was hidden

Compliance officers couldn't see which requirements applied to which jurisdiction. We designed a rules engine UI that surfaced requirements contextually.

Trust signals reduce verification anxiety

Investors uploading sensitive ID documents needed reassurance. Adding encryption badges, progress indicators, and clear data-handling messaging cut abandonment by 40%.

Design Decisions
01

Progressive disclosure over single-page forms

Considered

Single long form, tabbed wizard, progressive steps

Chose & Why

Progressive steps — analytics showed 60% drop-off on single forms. Step-based flow with save-and-resume achieved 85% completion. Users could see progress and return later without losing work.

Before/After comparison
02

Contextual jurisdiction rules over global settings

Considered

Global settings page, per-offering config, contextual inline rules

Chose & Why

Contextual inline rules — compliance officers needed to see which rules applied WHILE reviewing investors, not in a separate settings screen. Reduced context-switching by 70%.

Reflection
✓ What Worked

1. Progressive disclosure reduced cognitive load — investors completed one step at a time instead of facing a wall of forms

2. Jurisdiction-aware rules engine eliminated manual compliance checking — the system surfaced the right requirements automatically

3. Save-and-resume was the single biggest win — investors could leave mid-flow and return days later without losing progress

◈ What I'd Do Differently

1. Mobile-first from day one — we retrofitted mobile support years after desktop launch, which created layout debt

2. User testing earlier in the compliance flow — we relied too heavily on stakeholder interviews and not enough on watching real investors struggle
