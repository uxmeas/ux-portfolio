Deep Problem Framing
The Goal

AKMSecure is a startup in industrial cybersecurity — a space dominated by billion-dollar defense contractors. They needed a brand identity and web presence that communicated military-grade security while remaining approachable enough for mid-market industrial companies.

The Problem

A startup with no brand competing against established names like Fortinet, Palo Alto Networks, and CrowdStrike. Without a professional brand identity and website, potential clients dismissed them before the first conversation. The challenge was looking enterprise-ready on a startup budget.

How Might We

How might we create a brand that communicates military-grade security while remaining approachable — positioning a startup to compete with billion-dollar incumbents?

Before No brand No identity, no website, no visual language. Dismissed by enterprise prospects.
After Enterprise-ready Full brand system, responsive website, CMS integration, and print collateral.
Design Approach / Solution

I designed a complete brand identity system — logo, color palette, typography, iconography — that balances authority with approachability. The visual language uses dark backgrounds and sharp geometry to signal security, with warm accent colors and clear typography to remain human.

The responsive website was built for performance and SEO, with CMS integration so the client can update content independently. Print collateral extended the brand to business cards, presentations, and leave-behinds for enterprise sales meetings.

Deliverables

1. Brand identity — logo, colors, typography, iconography

2. Responsive website — mobile-first, performance-optimized

3. CMS integration — client-managed content updates

4. Print collateral — business cards, presentations, sales materials

Reflection
✓ What Worked

1. Authority-first visual language — dark backgrounds and sharp geometry immediately signaled "enterprise security" to potential clients

2. CMS integration — the client updates content independently, which means the site stays current without ongoing designer involvement

◈ What I'd Do Differently

1. Include case study templates in the CMS — the client needs to showcase wins but building case study pages requires design help each time

2. Design a sales deck template — print collateral was useful but a presentation template would have been the most-used deliverable
