Deep Problem Framing
The Goal

Arts organizations in Calgary needed a branded fundraising platform that supported tax receipts, curated project submissions, and donation matching. Kickstarter and Indiegogo didn't offer any of these. We set out to build a white-label crowdfunding platform purpose-built for local arts communities.

The Problem

There was no local crowdfunding platform for arts organizations. Artists used Kickstarter, which meant no tax receipts for donors, no curation by arts councils, and no branding control. Donors had no incentive beyond rewards — and large philanthropic donations weren't happening because there was no tax benefit. Arts councils had zero visibility into campaign performance or fund distribution.

How Might We

How might we build a curated crowdfunding platform that connects local artists with community donors — with donation matching, tax receipts, and full branding control?

User Research
Methodology

Interviews with independent artists, arts council administrators, and community donors. Observation of existing crowdfunding behavior on Kickstarter and Indiegogo. Analysis of donation patterns and tax receipt usage in the Calgary arts community.

Sample

15+ artists across disciplines, 6 arts council administrators, 20+ community donors. Research conducted over 6 months prior to platform launch.

Key Insights
Tax receipts drive large donations

Donors over $100 consistently cited tax receipts as the deciding factor. Without them, philanthropic-level donations didn't happen on general platforms like Kickstarter.

Matching multiplies momentum

When ATB Financial matched donations, campaigns hit their goals 3x faster. $50K was matched in just 2 months — creating urgency that drove both new and repeat donors.

Curation builds trust but slows launch

Arts council approval gave donors confidence that projects were legitimate. But the approval process took up to 1 month — and some artists went to Kickstarter rather than wait.

Design Approach / Solution

Our team designed and co-built a white-label crowdfunding platform that gave arts organizations full control over their fundraising experience — from branded campaign pages to curated project submissions and automated tax receipts.

The platform included a donation matching engine that partnered with financial institutions like ATB Financial, creating urgency and amplifying donor impact. Every campaign was curated by the arts council before going live, building trust with donors.

Key Features

1. White-label campaign pages with full branding control

2. Curated submission workflow with arts council approval

3. Donation matching engine (ATB Financial partnership)

4. Electronic tax receipts for Canadian donors

5. Admin dashboard for arts organizations

Reflection
✓ What Worked

1. Donation matching created urgency — ATB Financial's match drove $50K in 2 months, proving that institutional partnerships amplify grassroots campaigns

2. Tax receipts unlocked philanthropic giving — donors consistently gave 5-10x more when they received a tax benefit, turning $50 donors into $500 donors

3. White-label branding built institutional trust — arts organizations presented the platform as their own, which increased donor confidence

◈ What I'd Do Differently

1. Faster approval process — the 1-month curation cycle was too long. Artists went to Kickstarter rather than wait. I'd design a 48-hour fast-track for returning creators

2. Faster fund distribution — a 3-month wait for funds was unacceptable for artists who needed money to create. I'd design milestone-based payouts

3. The platform closed in 2016 — competing on usability was a losing battle against Kickstarter's scale. I should have doubled down on the unique value (tax receipts, matching, curation) instead of trying to match feature parity
