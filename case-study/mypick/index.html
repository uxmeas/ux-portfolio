MyPick.io — Visual Elimination Randomizer

Role Product Designer & Developer
Timeline 2025–2026
Type Web App · Randomizer
Tools Vanilla JS, Supabase Realtime, Canvas
Hero Image — MyPick.io Race in Progress
Deep Problem Framing
The Goal

Random selection tools are boring. Spinning wheels, random.org, coin flips — they produce a result but create no engagement. I wanted to make random selection feel like entertainment — the suspense of a race, the engagement of a shared experience, the utility of a decision tool.

The Problem

Existing randomizers are solitary and instant. There's no build-up, no shared experience, no reason to watch. Teachers picking students, teams making decisions, friends choosing restaurants — all these scenarios benefit from suspense and participation, but no tool delivers it.

How Might We

How might we make random selection feel like entertainment — with the suspense of a race, the engagement of shared participation, and the utility of a real decision tool?

Before Boring Spinning wheels, random.org — instant result, zero engagement, no shared experience.
After Event Visual race with sound, dynamic camera, and real-time multiplayer rooms.
User Research
Key Insights
01 Suspense is the core mechanic

The value isn't the result — it's the anticipation. A 15-120 second race creates emotional investment in the outcome. The longer the race, the more the audience cares who wins.

02 Multiplayer transforms engagement

When a group watches the same race in real time, it becomes a shared experience. Room codes let anyone join with a 6-character code — no accounts, no downloads, instant participation.

03 Sound is 50% of the experience

24 sound effects — crowd cheers, engine revs, collision impacts, victory fanfares — transform a visual animation into an immersive experience. Muting the sound cuts the emotional impact in half.

Design Approach / Solution

MyPick.io uses an elimination race mechanic — teams race on screen, and the visual spectacle creates suspense around what's ultimately a random selection. The dynamic camera follows the race leader, creating a broadcast-quality viewing experience.

Multiplayer rooms use 6-character codes via Supabase Realtime. The host controls the race while viewers watch live with 100% sync accuracy. Billboards, weather effects, and night mode add visual variety across races.

Key Features

1. 25 teams, 15-120 second configurable race duration

2. Dynamic camera following race leader

3. Multiplayer rooms with 6-character codes

4. 24 integrated sound effects

5. Billboards, weather effects, and night mode

Reflection
✓ What Worked

1. Emotion-first design — building for suspense and excitement rather than pure utility created a product people actually enjoy using

2. Authoritative host pattern — the host controls the race state and viewers mirror it, which solved sync issues completely

3. Sound design investment — 24 effects seems excessive but sound is what makes the experience feel alive

◈ What I'd Do Differently

1. Mobile-first layout — the race view was designed for projectors/screens and needed retrofitting for phone viewers in multiplayer rooms

2. Analytics from day one — no telemetry means no data on how people actually use races (duration preferences, team counts, replay behavior)
