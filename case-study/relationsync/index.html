RelationSync — AI Voice Journal for Therapists

Hero Image — RelationSync Mobile App Screens
Deep Problem Framing
The Goal

Therapists typing notes during or after sessions disrupts the therapeutic connection. The act of documentation competes with the act of being present. I explored how voice capture with AI structuring could eliminate this tension entirely.

The Problem

Session documentation is a clinical requirement but a therapeutic liability. Typing during sessions breaks eye contact and emotional attunement. Typing after sessions relies on memory and eats into personal time. Therapists need to document without compromising presence.

How Might We

How might we help therapists document sessions without disrupting connection — using voice instead of typing, with AI structuring notes into clinical records?

Before Typing notes Disrupts therapeutic flow. Breaks eye contact. Relies on memory after sessions.
After Voice capture AI transcribes and structures voice notes into client records. Presence preserved.
Design Approach / Solution

RelationSync centers on a voice journal — therapists speak their observations naturally, and AI transcribes, categorizes, and structures the content into clinical records. A guided onboarding flow introduces the concept gradually to therapists unfamiliar with AI tools.

The design system prioritizes calm — soft colors, generous spacing, and minimal cognitive load. Every interaction is designed for the transitional moments between sessions when therapists have 2-3 minutes to capture observations.

Key Features

1. Voice journal with AI transcription and structuring

2. Guided onboarding for non-technical therapists

3. Client records organized by session and theme

4. Calming design system for clinical context

Reflection
✓ What Worked

1. Voice-first interaction model — removed the keyboard entirely from the documentation workflow, preserving therapeutic presence

2. Calming design system — clinical tools often feel clinical; this design felt like a wellness app, reducing adoption resistance

◈ What I'd Do Differently

1. Validate with real therapists earlier — concept testing with clinicians would have surfaced HIPAA and privacy concerns that shaped the design

2. Prototype the AI structuring logic — the value proposition depends entirely on AI accuracy, which needs validation beyond design
