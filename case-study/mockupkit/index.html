Deep Problem Framing
The Goal

Designers need device mockups for portfolios, presentations, and client deliverables — but creating them means leaving Figma for Photoshop or third-party mockup tools. Each mockup takes 5-10 minutes and breaks the design workflow.

The Problem

Existing mockup tools either require leaving Figma entirely or produce flat, lifeless results without perspective. The top Figma plugins have millions of users but limited perspective control — they offer presets, not creative freedom.

How Might We

How might we create perspective-accurate device mockups without leaving Figma — in under 30 seconds?

Before 5-10 min per mockup Leave Figma, open Photoshop or Smartmockups, export, reimport. Workflow broken.
After 30 seconds in Figma Select frame, pick device, adjust perspective, done. Never leave Figma.
User Research
Key Insights
Perspective is the differentiator

Flat mockups look amateur. Real perspective transforms a screenshot into a professional presentation piece. DLT homography math enables 4-point perspective that competitors don't offer.

Designers want control, not presets

Fixed mockup angles feel limiting. Designers need to drag perspective points freely to match their composition — not pick from a grid of predetermined views.

Freemium validates demand

Offering core devices for free and premium devices at $8 lets designers try before buying. Free users become advocates, and the price point is low enough for impulse purchase.

Design Approach / Solution

MockupKit uses DLT (Direct Linear Transform) homography to map screen content onto device frames with mathematically accurate perspective. Designers drag 4 corner points to set any angle — not limited to presets.

The plugin generates native Figma frames, not flattened images. Everything stays editable. Device colors, landscape orientation, and quantity are all one-click controls.

Key Features

1. 5 device templates (iPhone, MacBook, iPad, Android, Desktop)

2. 4-point perspective editor with free-form dragging

3. Device color options and landscape toggle

4. Quantity stepper for multi-device compositions

Reflection
✓ What Worked

1. DLT homography — the math-based perspective approach created a genuine differentiator against preset-only competitors

2. Freemium model — free core devices lowered the barrier while the $8 Pro price felt like an impulse buy for working designers

◈ What I'd Do Differently

1. More device templates at launch — 5 devices covers the basics but designers immediately asked for watches, TVs, and custom frames

2. Batch mockup generation — designers creating portfolios need the same screen in multiple devices at once
