Investor Onboarding for Private Capital Markets

Reduced a 45-minute KYC flow to 12 minutes. 85% completion. 150+ offerings across IIROC, Reg CF, MiFID, and FINTRAC.

( 01 ) The Problem

60% drop-off at document upload before the redesign. Investors were asked to upload 6 to 8 documents in a single session with no save-and-resume. Every jurisdiction had different requirements (IIROC, Regulation CF, MiFID, ISA, FINTRAC), but the UI treated a California accredited investor and a UK retail investor identically. Compliance officers had no dashboard visibility into who was stuck, who had abandoned, and why. The platform was processing private placements for Canaccord Genuity, Raymond James, and Sprott Capital. The result was a system that was compliant on paper but commercially broken in practice.

( 02 ) How It Was Framed

Research happened through live onboarding observation sessions across Canaccord, Raymond James, and Sprott deal flows, plus drop-off analytics from the platform's own data. The work spanned multiple jurisdictions where regulatory requirements diverged (IIROC accreditation rules differ from Regulation CF crowdfunding rules differ from MiFID appropriateness testing). The pattern was consistent across every jurisdiction: investors didn't abandon because the process was hard. They abandoned because the process gave them no information, no progress, and no way to pause.

HMW

How might we reduce investor onboarding from 45 to 12 minutes while maintaining KYC/AML compliance across 20+ regulatory jurisdictions including IIROC, Regulation CF, MiFID, ISA, and FINTRAC?

( 03 ) The Answer
A / 01

Progressive steps eliminated the wall of forms

The original flow presented 6 to 8 documents at once. A compliance checklist dressed as a user experience. Three approaches were evaluated: a single long form, a tabbed wizard, and progressive disclosure with save-and-resume. The platform's own drop-off data made the choice. 60% abandonment at the document upload step. Progressive steps with save-and-resume shifted that to 85% completion. Investors could complete identity verification on Monday and return to financial accreditation on Wednesday. Progress persisted. Drop-off didn't.

A / 02

Contextual jurisdiction rules replaced the global settings model

Compliance officers were spending hours in separate settings screens configuring requirements before reviewing investors. The redesign moved those rules inline, surfacing the exact documents and thresholds required for each investor's jurisdiction directly in the review interface. The compliance officer reviewing a FINTRAC investor saw FINTRAC rules. The one reviewing a MiFID investor saw MiFID rules. The IIROC provincial accreditation matrix, the Regulation CF US funding portal rules, and the ISA UK annual limits were all surfaced contextually.

A / 03

Trulioo and Onfido integration automated identity verification end to end

KYC identity verification needed to handle investors in 20+ jurisdictions without asking compliance officers to know each country's document formats. Trulioo (global identity verification across 195 countries) covered the data layer. Onfido (document scan plus selfie biometric) covered the document layer. The team designed fallback paths for both: automated when the confidence score cleared threshold, manual review when it didn't. Compliance officers saw a single queue instead of 20+ country-specific backlogs.

A / 04

NIGO document reduction validated the end-to-end redesign

The combined effect across the platform: 98% reduction in NIGO (not-in-good-order) documents across the deal lifecycle. Subscription documents that previously arrived wrong (missing signatures, expired accreditation, wrong jurisdiction) were now auto-validated at the upload step. ECM teams stopped chasing document corrections and started closing deals.

( 04 ) Key Screen Annotations
SCREEN 01

KYC Start Screen

The opening screen doesn't ask for anything. It explains what's needed, how long it takes, and what happens to the data before a single document is uploaded. Trust is established before compliance begins.

SCREEN 02

Compliance Dashboard

Compliance officers see every investor's exact status across every jurisdiction. Not just "complete" or "incomplete" but which step they're on, what's blocking them, when they last logged in, and which framework applies (IIROC, Reg CF, MiFID, ISA). The dashboard replaced a spreadsheet workflow that required manual chasing.

SCREEN 03

Jurisdiction Rules Engine

Configuration UI where admins defined which documents, accreditation thresholds, and appropriateness tests applied per jurisdiction. Compliance officers never touched this screen. Investors never saw it. It worked silently in the background, adapting every onboarding flow to the right regulatory regime.

( 05 ) Results

45 to 12 min onboarding · 85% completion rate · 98% NIGO document reduction · $2B+ processed · 150+ offerings · 20+ jurisdictions (IIROC, Regulation CF, MiFID, ISA, FINTRAC)

( 06 ) Reflection
What worked

Progressive disclosure reduced cognitive load in a domain where cognitive load directly costs money. The jurisdiction-aware rules engine is the feature compliance officers cite most often. Save-and-resume was the single biggest behavioral change. It turned a high-stakes single session into a returnable process. Trulioo and Onfido integration scaled identity verification across 20+ jurisdictions without a 20x headcount increase on the compliance team.

What to do differently

Mobile-first from day one. The platform was retrofitted for mobile years after desktop launch, creating layout debt that took multiple sprints to address. Earlier investor-side usability testing. The research relied heavily on compliance officer observation, and spending equal time with the end investors (not just the admins watching them) would have surfaced friction sooner.
