The Problem

Investment dealers at Canaccord, Raymond James, Echelon, and Sprott were managing private placements through email chains and spreadsheets. ECM teams spent weeks chasing documents instead of closing deals.

Everything was done through email, so there was just a lot of room for error and things took way longer than they needed to.
Canaccord Genuity · describing the pre-platform state

40% of subscription agreements arrived with errors at quality check stage. Wrong amounts. Missing signatures. Expired accreditation.

Every handoff across the deal lifecycle (offering creation, onboarding, compliance, subscription, funding, distribution) was a manual spreadsheet or email.

( 02 )
How It Was Framed

Research surfaced the pattern across every dealer: the platform's value wasn't any single feature. It was the elimination of every handoff.

Dealers weren't paying for subscription agreement software or for offering wizards. They were paying for one source of truth that every stakeholder (ECM team, compliance, wealth management, legal, issuer, investor) could trust.

The team framed the problem across three structural needs: issuers needed to launch offerings without waiting weeks for legal setup. Investors needed transparency into deal status and portfolio performance. Compliance needed every transaction auditable end to end.

HMW

How might we replace email-based private placement management with an end-to-end platform that reduces close time by 48% and eliminates document errors, for investment dealers operating across multiple regulatory jurisdictions?

( 03 )
The Answer
A / 01

End-to-end beat best-of-breed

Competitors handled pieces of the deal. The team chose to build across the full lifecycle. Offering creation through funding, distribution, and ongoing investor reporting. Every stakeholder worked from the same source of truth. That architectural choice is what drove 97% customer retention through 2023 (3% quarterly churn per board reporting). Dealers didn't leave because leaving meant rebuilding their entire workflow across five vendors.

A / 02

DealFlow Mesh connected the industry

DealFlow Mesh launched 2023 as the industry's first private capital network. Real-time book building across ECM teams, wealth management, law firms, and issuers. Backed by $510K in NRC IRAP funding ($350K in 2022, $160K in 2023). The team designed the coordination UX across a network where every participant had different priorities, different workflows, and different regulatory obligations.

A / 03

Token-based white-labeling served 154 clients

Every dealer needed to present the platform as their own branded experience. Building custom designs per client would have been commercially impossible. Design tokens handled brand adaptation. Logos, colors, and typography switched per deployment while the underlying component architecture never forked. One system. 154 implementations across 17 Enterprise tier, 50+ Pro tier, and 10+ Starter tier clients. 85+ partnerships across 6 continents by Q2 2018.

( 04 )
Key Screen Annotations

Investor Dashboard

Investors see their full portfolio across all holdings, not just the current offering. Distributions, documents, and deal status in one view. This replaced the standard approach of one email per event.

Deal Management View

ECM teams see every offering's status across all investors simultaneously. Funding progress, compliance completion, pending subscriptions, and upcoming closing deadlines. The dashboard that replaced a wall of spreadsheets.

DealFlow Mesh Book

Real-time book building interface where dealers saw commitment levels, allocation status, and counterparty activity as it happened. Previously a multi-day reconciliation process. Now a live canvas.

Reflection
What worked

Building the full lifecycle in one platform created a competitive moat that individual feature competitors couldn't match. Token-based white-labeling proved that design systems are also business decisions. 154 implementations with zero per-client design overhead.

Growing design practice from 3-person startup to public company with design at the table demonstrated that UX leadership drives revenue in enterprise fintech.

The design work also outlasted the company: PropertyBridges.com (P2P property investment, Central Bank of Ireland regulated, 8,000+ investors), WeAreStarting.it (Italian equity crowdfunding, 2,500+ users), and MaRS VX (Toronto innovation hub venture exchange) are all still operational years after launch.

What to do differently

A formalized design system from year one, not year five. The first five years were ad hoc components that created consistency debt requiring full audits before each major client launch.

Earlier structured user research. The founding team relied on intuition and client feedback for too long before establishing formal research practices.

Cleaner separation between DealFlow Accounts (investor-facing onboarding) and DealFlow Core (admin-facing deal management) from the product architecture level. The two workflows got tangled in the same information architecture for years before being split.
